AI in HBO-ICT; an evolution or a threat?

    9 September 2025

    Whether or not the use of AI in HBO-ICT threatens it or urges it to change.

    The role of AI within the IT

    Within IT, various forms of AI are used for different fields of IT. In data science, AI is often used for data prediction and analysis. In surveillance settings, AI is often used in camera systems to detect people, vehicles and the flow of both. As of recently, AI is being adopted in coding environments for problems that people can't easily solve themselves, or simply as a coding assistant or code reviewing tool. Yes, people even generate images and videos using AI, although those results tend to look 'off' to most.

    The usage of AI within HBO-ICT

    Given AI's quickly growing ability to code quite well, more people at HBO-ICT use AI for problem-solving. This in itself isn't a problem. However, some of these students let AI do most of their work without actually understanding the meaning of the generated code. It's good to keep in mind that AI makes mistakes, hallucinates quite often and also tends to pick up bad coding habits.

    How I think AI should be used within HBO-ICT

    When using AI, one should keep in mind to use it as a tool, not as a replacement for your work to prevent potentially unsafe and/or problematic code from appearing. Also, when using AI in coding, make sure to understand the code it (re)wrote for you. After all, relying too heavily on what is essentially a black box of hard-to-predict digital intelligence can turn out wrong in settings where good code really matters, like a company's website backend.

    How HBO-ICT could move forwards using AI

    Inherently, AI usage doesn't have to be bad. As long as you know what your codebase means, what it does and its flaws, then you're good. However, to prevent AI from becoming a problem within this program, teacher can apply various methods. These include, but aren't limited to...

    • Informing students of the risks and the rewards of using AI in coding

    • Designing coding tests that AI assistants tend to be bad at, discouraging overuse of AI
